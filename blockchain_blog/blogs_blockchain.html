What is Blockchain?

A beginner-friendly introduction to blockchain technology

Introduction

Blockchain is a distributed digital ledger technology that records data in a secure, transparent, and tamper-resistant way. Instead of storing information in a single centralized database, blockchain stores data across multiple connected blocks.

How Blockchain Works

Blockchain works by grouping data into blocks. Each block contains a list of transactions, a cryptographic hash, and the hash of the previous block. These blocks are linked together to form a chain, making it extremely difficult to alter any stored information.

Key Components of Blockchain

  • Block: A container that stores data.
  • Hash: A unique digital fingerprint of a block.
  • Previous Hash: Connects blocks together.
  • Chain: A sequence of linked blocks.

Why Blockchain is Secure

Blockchain uses cryptographic hashing to secure data. If someone tries to change the information inside a block, its hash will change, breaking the link with the next block. This makes data tampering easily detectable.

Blockchain vs Traditional Databases

Traditional databases are centralized and controlled by a single authority, whereas blockchain is decentralized and distributed. This decentralization improves transparency and trust.

Real-World Applications

Blockchain is widely used in cryptocurrencies, supply chain management, digital identity verification, and certificate authentication systems.

Conclusion

Blockchain is a powerful technology that enhances security, transparency, and trust in digital systems. Understanding its fundamentals is essential for exploring modern decentralized applications.
