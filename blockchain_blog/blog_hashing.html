What is Hashing?

Understanding cryptographic hashing in blockchain

Introduction

Hashing is a cryptographic technique used to convert input data into a fixed-length string of characters. In blockchain, hashing plays a crucial role in maintaining data integrity and security.

What is a Hash?

A hash is a unique digital fingerprint generated from data. Even a small change in the input produces a completely different hash value.

Role of Hashing in Blockchain

  • Secures block data
  • Links blocks together
  • Detects data tampering
  • Ensures immutability

Example of Hashing

If a certificate’s data is hashed and stored in a block, any attempt to modify that certificate will change its hash, breaking the blockchain link.

Why Hashing is Important

Hashing ensures that blockchain data remains secure and verifiable without revealing the actual content.

Conclusion

Hashing is the backbone of blockchain security. It ensures that data stored in a blockchain cannot be altered without detection.
